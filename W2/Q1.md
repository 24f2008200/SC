## Registered Ports Range

Registered ports fall within the range of 1024 to 49151. These ports are also known as user ports and are assigned by the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority (IANA) for specific applications and services upon request. This distinguishes them from well-known ports (0-1023) and dynamic/private ports (49152-65535) [Service Name and Transport Protocol Port Number Registry](cite://https://www.iana.org/assignments/service-names-port-numbers)[Registered port - Wikipedia](cite://https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_port).

### Details
#### Background / Context
In computer networking, port numbers are 16-bit values that identify specific processes or services on a network host. The total range of port numbers is from 0 to 65535. These ports are categorized into three main types: well-known ports, registered ports, and dynamic/private ports [Service Name and Transport Protocol Port Number Registry](cite://https://www.iana.org/assignments/service-names-port-numbers).

#### Comparison / Options
The three main categories of TCP and UDP port numbers are:
-   **Well-Known Ports (0-1023)**: These are also known as system ports and are reserved for commonly used services and applications, such as HTTP (port 80), HTTPS (port 443), FTP (port 21), and SSH (port 22) [List of TCP and UDP port numbers - Wikipedia](cite://https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_TCP_and_UDP_port_numbers)[PortReference - Wireshark Wiki](cite://https://wiki.wireshark.org/PortReference). They are used by system processes that provide widely accessible network services [List of TCP and UDP port numbers - Wikipedia](cite://https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_TCP_and_UDP_port_numbers).
-   **Registered Ports (1024-49151)**: These ports are assigned by IANA for specific user applications and services. They are not as tightly controlled as well-known ports but are registered to avoid conflicts [Service Name and Transport Protocol Port Number Registry](cite://https://www.iana.org/assignments/service-names-port-numbers).
-   **Dynamic/Private Ports (49152-65535)**: Also known as ephemeral ports, these are not assigned by IANA and are typically used by client applications when initiating connections. They are dynamically allocated by the operating system for temporary use and are released after the connection is closed [Service Name and Transport Protocol Port Number Registry](cite://https://www.iana.org/assignments/service-names-port-numbers)[PortReference - Wireshark Wiki](cite://https://wiki.wireshark.org/PortReference).
